Why it matters

Most blind companies don’t think about your room.
They think about your order.

Large chains send whoever is available. Measurements are delegated. Fitting is subcontracted. When something is wrong, accountability is hard to find.

Made-to-measure blinds cannot be returned.

A wrong measurement or poor fitting isn’t a minor inconvenience — it means replacing the entire product. The difference between a careful independent specialist and a national chain often only becomes clear when something goes wrong.

01

The wrong measurement costs you the whole blind

Made-to-measure products are cut to your exact specification. A measurement error — even by 5mm — means the blind won’t fit, won’t hang straight, or won’t close properly. National companies delegate surveys to third-party contractors with no stake in the outcome.

02

Subcontracted fitting means no accountability

When your fitter isn’t employed by the company you bought from, complaints become circular. The brand points at the fitter. The fitter has moved on. We employ every person who comes into your home — so accountability begins and ends with us.

03

Generic product ranges don’t fit London homes

London properties have non-standard windows: bay windows, sash frames, deep reveals, unusual angles. National catalogues are designed for new-build proportions. We have fitted 4,000+ London homes and understand what every postcode typically needs.
